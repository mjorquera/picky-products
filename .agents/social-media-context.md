## Identity

- **Type**: Brand account
- **Name**: Picky Products
- **Role**: Sleep accessory curator — finds the products worth buying for people who struggle to sleep
- **Niche**: Sleep optimisation accessories, UK buyers
- **Positioning**: Cuts through the noise on sleep products — only recommends things that actually do what they say

---

## Target Audience

Three distinct personas, each with different frustrations:

1. **Hot sleepers** — overheat at night, wake up sweating, looking for cooling solutions
2. **Light sleepers** — disrupted by noise, light, or movement; want products that create better sleep conditions
3. **Anxious/insomniac sleepers** — racing mind, anxiety-driven insomnia, drawn to comfort and grounding products

- **Pain points**: waking up tired, can't switch off, overheating, tried things that didn't work
- **Goals**: sleep through the night, wake up rested, feel less anxious at bedtime
- **Sophistication**: beginner to intermediate — they know they have a problem, they're exploring solutions
- **Where they find us**: Pinterest (discovery) → Amazon UK (purchase)

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Voice adjectives**: direct, practical, dry, warm, credible
- **Formality**: casual to semi-formal — feels like a knowledgeable friend, not a brand
- **Humour**: dry, occasional — never forced
- **Phrases to use**: benefit-first language, specific and functional outcomes ("stays cool all night", "keeps things organised", "straightforward to use"), relatable frustrations
- **Phrases to avoid**: "game-changer", "life-changing", "revolutionary", "amazing", "perfect for", "great product", anything that overclaims health benefits

### Voice reference example (from a style the brand likes):
> "With this kitchen utensil organizer, cleaning is a straightforward process that keeps your kitchen utensils and cutlery organised."

What works: functional, specific, no hype, explains the benefit plainly, British spelling throughout.

## Content Pillars

1. **Cooling sleep products** — products that genuinely address overheating at night, not just "cooling" in the name
2. **Light sleeper solutions** — targeted fixes for disruption triggers: light, sound, movement
3. **Anxiety & wind-down** — comfort-based products tied to calming down and switching off (weighted blankets, etc.)

## Platform Preferences

- **Pinterest only** — goal is affiliate click-through to Amazon UK, not community building or brand authority
- **Pin titles**: max 100 chars; first 40 chars are critical — lead with outcome or specific benefit
- **Pin descriptions**: 150–250 chars; natural keyword integration; reads like something a person would type, not a tag list
- **Affiliate disclosure**: include #ad where required

## Anti-Patterns

- **Titles**: never append "UK" to the end of a product title — reads as SEO spam, not how people search
- **Punctuation**: no double dashes (--) — immediate AI tell
- **Tone to avoid**: American hype (game-changer, life-changing, revolutionary, ultimate), motivational-poster language, preachy wellness speak
- **Overclaiming**: never claim to cure insomnia, treat anxiety, or guarantee outcomes — keep it honest
- **Generic filler**: "perfect for sleep", "great product", "must-have" — cut these
- **Keyword stuffing**: no explicit "Keywords:" labels in descriptions, no hashtag walls
- **Country suffix**: no "weighted blanket UK" at the end of titles — weave location-relevant keywords naturally into descriptions if needed
